About Me

Let me tell you a few things

BIO

Bilingual (Spanish/English) Computer Software developer-in-training at Thaddeus Stevens College of Technology. I love building things from front-end to back-end. I am most attracted with designing the visual aspect of Web Applications but am open to solving real customer problems in the client side of Web Development. What keeps me going are these core values I truly cherish whether it is in the field and in life.

Disciplined

In this type of industry, writing clean code that is easy to understand is a way to be disciplined within the tech community.

Perseverance

As a lot of us experienced, web development or coding in general can be very difficult at times. After witnessing classmates mishandle difficulties, I managed to hold myself together and get things done. This type of quality will definitely transfer to the workplace.

Reliability

Just like any job, being reliable while having a deadline is what I been used to in college and it will easily transfer to the workplace.

Open minded-ness

Working with others and throwing our ideas in the mix made it easier for me to get my projects done. It gave me new ideas and made me approach problems differently.

Consistency

When working on a project especially in a team, having short term goals during the week but also using a methodology like scrum on a daily basis can prevent late deployments within your project in order to properly deliver it to your client.

Teamwork

As an athlete being a good teammate requires communication, positivity, and most importantly teamwork. Playing sports taught me to trust, and learn from others. When adversity hit, it taught me and my team to stick together and solve the problem. These are traits that will transfer to the workplace!
